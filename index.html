The Challenge

More Than Just a Crossing

For too long, the intersection at 500 South and Guardsman Way has stood as a barrier—a dangerous crossing that challenges pedestrians, cyclists, and drivers alike. This isn't just about traffic; it's about lives.

We need to solve the longstanding traffic and safety challenge at the southern gateway to the University. The goal: reduce fatal accidents, honor the legacy of Jeff, the bridge's founder, and transform a barrier into a bridge that connects our community.

Safety Crisis

A high-risk intersection that puts pedestrians and cyclists in danger daily

Campus Disconnection

South campus remains isolated from the main campus and the vibrant U Nation

Traffic Congestion

Over 25,000 daily trips create bottlenecks and frustration for everyone

The Story Behind the Vision

Jeff's Mission

One rainy night changed everything—but the vision for a safer community had been years in the making.

Jeff Creveling

Founder, Ute Bridge Initiative

October 11th, 2025 — A rainy night outside Rice-Eccles Stadium during the Utes vs. Arizona State Sun Devils game.

Jeff was riding his bike through the 500 South and Guardsman intersection when he was struck by a car.

Rushed to the Emergency Room, Jeff's life changed forever. The physical and emotional pain left a lasting impact.

The Vision Was Already There. Jeff had been planning the Ute Bridge for years—driven by a belief that our community deserved better infrastructure.

But that night, the mission became personal. Jeff's goal is clear: ensure that no one else suffers the same pain he did.

The Solution

Innovation Meets Tradition

The Ute Bridge isn't just infrastructure—it's an architectural landmark that celebrates the spirit of the U Nation while solving real problems.

Four-Corner Engagement

Unlike typical overpasses, the Ute Bridge connects all four corners equally—creating a true hub that serves every direction seamlessly.

The "Block U" Design

Accessible ramps for bikes, scooters, and wheelchairs form the iconic "Block U" when viewed from the sky—a symbol visible to all.

Flashing the U to the World

The bridge architecture physically mimics the famous U hand gesture, welcoming all visitors passing beneath with pride.

Gathering Space

An elevated, canopied platform offering 360-degree views—a place to pause, connect, and take in the beauty of campus.

Rice-Eccles Echo

Built with red sandstone panels to match the stadium aesthetic—honoring tradition while building for the future.

Fully Accessible

Designed with gentle slopes and smooth surfaces for wheelchairs, strollers, bikes, and scooters—everyone is welcome.
